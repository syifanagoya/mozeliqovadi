    Alesha Dixon, a British dancer and singer, claims to have more than £2 million. Alesha Dixon co-founded Mis-Teeq with Su-Elise Nash and Zena McNally in 1999.

    They then released the single “Why” in 2001, with the song gaining popularity with the release of a garage remix that catapulted it to number eight on the charts.

    Their second song, “All I Want,” reached number two on the charts. Mis-Teeq released their self-titled first album, Lickin’ On Both Sides, in October of that year. In 2005, the group decided to split.

    Alesha Dixon Career

    Dixon signed a deal with Asylum Records in 2008, and the following November, The Alesha Show was released. “The Boy Does Nothing,” the lead song from her debut album, peaked at number five on the UK charts and was certified Gold.

    Her second single, “Breathe Slowly,” reached number three in the United Kingdom.

    The Alesha Show was certified Platinum by the BPI following the release of her third song, “Let’s Get Excited.” Dixon was announced as a judge on Strictly Come Dancing in September 2009 and later released her fourth song, “To Love Again.” That year, she also created her jewelry collection.
